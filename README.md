# The Last Frame

A minimalist 2D platformer about preserving memories before a system is lost forever.

## Overview

You are a glowing cursor trapped inside an old computer that is being formatted. In the next 60 seconds, everything will be erased. Your only goal: collect the three Memory Fragments—digital photographs that hold the most precious moments of a human life—and upload them to the Cloud before it's too late.

The game is not about fighting back. It's about remembrance. It's about what we choose to preserve.

## Features

✨ **Minimalist Visual Style**
- High-contrast black background with geometric shapes
- Simple white glowing player character with shadow effects
- Colorful memory fragments that stand out from the fading world
- Progressive desaturation effect as you advance through levels

⏱️ **Time Pressure**
- 60-second formatting progress bar that relentlessly advances
- Each level must be completed before time runs out
- The urgency creates emotional weight to your choices

📖 **Narrative Through Gameplay**
- In-game text messages reveal pieces of a human life
- Memory fragments are not abstract—they represent real moments
- The ending shifts from desperation to hope and preservation

🎮 **Core Mechanics**
- Simple horizontal movement and jumping
- Collect Memory Fragments to progress
- Avoid hazards and the void
- Platforms progressively disappear and environments decay

## How to Play

### Controls
- **Move Left**: Arrow Left or A
- **Move Right**: Arrow Right or D
- **Jump**: Space Bar, Arrow Up, or W

### Objective
1. Collect the Memory Fragment on each level
2. Reach Level 3 and collect the final fragment
3. Upload all three before the formatting completes (60 second limit)
4. Refresh the page to play again

### Levels

**Level 1: The Desktop**
- A vast, familiar landscape filled with corrupted obstacles
- Narrative: *"A familiar place, corrupted. Find what remains before it's gone forever."*
- Memory Fragment 1: A child's laugh

**Level 2: The Fade**
- Colors drain into grayscale; platforms crumble
- Narrative: *"Colors drain away. Platforms crumble. Time is running out."*
- Memory Fragment 2: A loyal companion

**Level 3: The Void**
- Almost everything has vanished; only fragments remain
- Narrative: *"Everything is fading. Only memories remain. Make the final jump."*
- Memory Fragment 3: A moment of triumph

## Design Philosophy

**Minimalism Over Complexity**
- Simple shapes, no unnecessary decoration
- UI focused on essential information (timer, progress, memory count)
- Every visual element serves a purpose

**Emotional Design**
- Time pressure creates genuine tension
- Desaturation effect symbolizes digital decay
- Collecting memories feels like rescue
- The ending is hopeful, not tragic

**Accessibility**
- No combat or complex mechanics to learn
- Forgiving gameplay (reset on damage)
- Clear visual feedback for all actions
- Fast-paced but not overwhelming

## Gameplay Tips

1. **Master the jump timing** - Platforms are spaced perfectly for pixel-precise jumps
2. **Watch the progress bar** - More urgency = higher stakes
3. **Memorize the level layout** - Resets are fast; use them to learn
4. **Color stands out** - Follow the bright Memory Fragments
5. **Red triangles are hazards** - Avoid them at all costs

## Narrative Themes

"The Last Frame" explores preservation, loss, and digital mortality. In an age where data is constantly created and destroyed, what makes certain memories worth saving? The game answers: the ones that shaped us. The child who grew up, the pet who loved us unconditionally, the moment we achieved something we'd worked toward.

As formatted data vanishes forever with each passing second, the act of collection becomes sacred. By uploading those three memories to the Cloud—a persistent, intangible space—they transcend the physical computer's death.

The ending is hopeful: preservation is possible. Not of the system, not of everything, but of what truly matters.
